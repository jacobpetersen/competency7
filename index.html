Cortado

‚≠ê Site Favorite!

While Spain loves to take credit for the creation of the Cortado, those who know, know that the drink was perfected right here in America at a little place called Blue Bottle Coffee in San Francisco, CA. It was at this mystical little coffee shop that Steve Ford began serving the 1:1 ratio of espresso to steamed milk drink not as a weak single shot drink in a wire handled espresso glass like his European brethren would, but as bold and beautiful double shot in a hefty Gibraltar glass. From this glass, the drink adopted it's off menu name of "Gibraltar" at the shop. From it's humble beginnings to cult favorite, this site's favorite coffee drink can now be found across the country, and even the world, often served in a Gibraltar glass and decorated with a heart shaped foam art, just as Steve would have done it.

Other Coffeehouse Favorites

Espresso

Bitter and powerful this drink is straight to the point, made by simply forcing pressurized hot water through finely ground coffee beans and adding nothing else.

Espresso Con Panna

For those who want the full power of espresso, but don't enjoy all the bitterness, we introduce the espresso con panna! A shot of espresso with a little bit of whipped cream to add a creamy texture.

Macchiato

Similar in size and ingredients to a European style cortado, the only thing differentiating this drink is that instead of steamed milk it uses foam milk.

Flat White

Newly popular in America (thanks to it's addition to the Starbucks menu) this beverage is one of the most complex mixtures on the site with a 1:3:2 ratio of espresso to milk to foamed milk.

Cappuccino

A classic breakfast favorite, this drink consists of an espresso and foamed milk mixture at approximately a 1:3 ratio, respectively.

Cafe Au Lait

AKA French morning coffee, this delightful drink starts with a french-pressed coffee mixed with warm milk to the consumer's delight.
